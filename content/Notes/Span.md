#### Definition
The set of all possible [[Linear Combination|linear combinations]] of a list of vectors $v_{1},v_{2},\ldots,v_{m}\in V$ is called the span of $v_{1},\ldots,v_{m}$ and denoted $\operatorname{span}(v_{1},\ldots,v_{m})$. 

The span of the empty list is $\{ 0 \}$. 

If $V=\operatorname{span}(v_{1},\ldots,v_{m})$, we say that $v_{1},\ldots, v_{m}$ spans $V$.

#### Properties
- $\operatorname{span}(v_{1},\ldots,v_{m})$ is the smallest subspace of $V$ that contains all of $v_{1},\ldots, v_{m}$.

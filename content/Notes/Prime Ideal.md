#### Definition
Given an [[ideal]] $P \unlhd R$ ($R$ is a commutative [[ring]] with $1_{R}$), we say $P$ is prime if $p \neq R$ and $ab\in P \implies a \in P$ or $b \in P$ (i.e. if $ab\in P$, $a \not\in P$, then $b\in P$).

#### Examples

##### Prime Ideals of $\mathbb{Z}$
Prime ideals of $\mathbb{Z}$ are $(p) = p\mathbb{Z}$, $p_{\geq 2}$ prime integer.
###### Proof
If $p \in \mathbb{Z}$ is prime, $a,b \in \mathbb{Z}$, $an \in p\mathbb{Z}=(p) \implies p \mid ab \underset{\text{Euclid}}{\implies} p \mid a$ or $p \mid b \implies$ $a$ or $b$ are in $(p) \implies (p)$ prime. $(0) \unlhd \mathbb{Z}$ prime ideal since $0$ is the only [[zero-divisor]] in $\mathbb{Z}$.

#### Definition
We say that a sequence $(x_{n})_{n}$ is Cauchy if $\forall \varepsilon > 0$, $\exists N > 0$ such that,
$$
\left\lvert x_{n}-x_{m} \right\rvert < \varepsilon, \forall n,m > N.
$$
#### Proposition
A sequence $(x_{n})_{n}$ is convergent if and only if it is a Cauchy sequence.

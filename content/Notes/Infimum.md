#### Definition
We say that $m \in \mathbb{R}$ is the infimum of a set $S \subset \mathbb{R}$ if the following are true,
1) $m$ is a [[Minimum#Lower Bound|lower bound]] of $S$ (therefore $S$ is bounded below).
2) For any lower bound $m'$ of $S$, $m' \leq m$ ($m$ is unique).

$m$ is also called the greatest lower bound.

We denote the infimum of a set $S$, $\inf(S)$.

#### Proof
Consider $\varphi: \mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}_{p}$ defined by $\varphi(n)= n \bmod p$
$\phi: \mathbb{Z}[x] \to \mathbb{Z}_{p}[x]$ induced morphism

Assume $P(x)= A(x)B(x)$, may assume $A,B$ monic of positive degree. Then $\phi(P) = \phi(A) \cdot \phi(B)$ in $\mathbb{Z}_{p}[x]$ (field, euclidean domain, principal ideal domain, and unique factorization domain)...

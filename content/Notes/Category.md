#### Definition
A category $\mathrm{C}$ is a structure comprised of two classes: $\operatorname{Obj}(\mathrm{C})$, the objects of $\mathrm{C}$, and $\operatorname{Hom}(\mathrm{C})$, the morphisms between all objects in $\operatorname{Obj}(\mathrm{C})$, along with the operation $\circ$ which is composition of morphisms,
$$
\circ: \operatorname{Hom}(b,c) \times \operatorname{Hom}(a,b) \mapsto \operatorname{Hom}(a,c),

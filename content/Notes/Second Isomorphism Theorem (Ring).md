#definition #abstract-algebra/ring-theory 

#### Definition
For ideals $I,J \unlhd R$, then we have an isomorphism of rings (without unity):
$$
{^{\textstyle (I+J)}\big/_{\textstyle J}}\simeq {^{\textstyle I}\big/_{\textstyle I \cap J}}.
$$
An isomorphism is $F: {^{\textstyle I}\big/_{\textstyle I \cap J}}\to {^{\textstyle I+J}\big/_{\textstyle J}}$ given by $F(x+ I \cap J)= x+J$.


#### Explanation of Terms
$J \unlhd R$
$J \subseteq I + J \unlhd R$ is an ideal, so it is a subring (maybe without unity), therefore $J \unlhd I + J$ is an ideal, so ${^{\textstyle I+J}\big/_{\textstyle J}}$ is a ring (maybe without unity).

Similarly, $I \cap J \subseteq I \unlhd R \implies {^{\textstyle I}\big/_{\textstyle I \cap J}}$ is a ring (maybe without unity).

#### Definition
A function $f:I \to \mathbb{R}$, for an interval $I$, is called uniformly continuous on $I$ if $\forall \varepsilon > 0$, there exists $\delta >0$ so that $|f(x) - f(y)| < \varepsilon$ for all $x,y \in I$, such that $|x-y| < \delta$.

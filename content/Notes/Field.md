#### Definition
A field $K$ is a [[ring]] in which the addition and multiplication operations are commutative, there exists a multiplicative identity, $1_{K}$, and every element $a \in K$ has a multiplicative inverse $a ^{-1} \in K$ such that $a \cdot a^{-1} = 1_{K}$. Simply, a field is a commutative division ring.

Alternatively, we could say that a set $K$ is a field if it has well-defined operations of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division that behave similarly to those four operations in the rational numbers or the real numbers. 

#### Remark
All fields are domains.

#### Definition
A [[ring]] $(R, +, \cdot)$ is called a domain (integral domain) if $(0)\unlhd R$ is a [[prime ideal]]. 

Equivalently, $xy = 0 \implies x= 0$ or $y=0$.

Equivalently, the only [[zero-divisor]] in $R$ is $0_{R}=0$.

#### Examples
- $(\mathbb{Z}, +, \cdot), (\mathbb{Q},+, \cdot), (\mathbb{R},+,\cdot)$ are domains.
- For $n \geq 2$, $(\mathbb{Z}_{n}, +,\cdot)$ is a domain if and only if $n$ is a prime integer.

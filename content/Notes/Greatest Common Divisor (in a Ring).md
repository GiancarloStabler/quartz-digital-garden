#### Definition
Take a commutative ring $R$, and elements $a,b \in R$ with $b \neq 0$, then we say that $a$ is a *multiple* of $b$ if there exists some $x \in R$ such that $a=bx$, or alternatively that $b$ *divides* $a$, denoted $b \mid a$. Furthermore, we say a *greatest common divisor*, of two elements $a,b \in R$ is some element $d \in R$ such that $d \mid a$ and $d \mid b$, and if $d' \mid a$ and $d' \mid b$ then $d' \mid d$.

In different language, we have that given an ideal $I$ generated by $a,b \in R$, $d$ is the greatest common divisor of $a,b$ if
1) $I \subseteq (d)$, and
2) If $(d')$ is an ideal containing $I$, then $(d) \subseteq (d')$.

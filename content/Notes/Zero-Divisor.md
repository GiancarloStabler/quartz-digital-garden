#### Definition
For commutative [[ring]] $(R, +, \cdot)$ with $1_{R}$, $r\in R$ is called a zero-divisor if there exists $\varphi: R \to R$ given by $\varphi(x)=rx$ with $\varphi$ additive, $\ker \varphi = \{  x\in R \mid rx=0 \}$, and $\varphi$ not injective if and only if $\exists x \neq 0_{R}$, $rx=0$.

#### Remark
If $R$ is finite, then every $r\in R$ is either a zero-divisor, or a unit.

#definition #abstract-algebra/ring-theory 

Ring ideals are the analogue of [[Normal Subgroup|normal subgroups]].
#### Definition
For [[ring]] $(R, +, \cdot)$, and $I \leq R$ a subring (not usually unital), we say that $I$ is a left ideal (denoted $I \unlhd R$) if 
$$
\forall x \in I, \forall r \in R,\quad rx \in I,
$$
$I$ is a right ideal if
$$
\forall x \in I, \forall r \in R, \quad xr \in I,
$$
or $I$ is a two sided ideal if
$$
\forall x \in I, \forall r\in R,\quad rx, xr \in I.
$$
If $R$ is commutative, these are all equivalent and we can simply call $I$ an ideal.
